CTF Challenge: Threat Intelligence Mind Map

Objective

Your mission is to identify the top 5 enemy nations of India based on open-source intelligence (OSINT) and geopolitical analysis. For each country, compile a list of their government websites and identify vulnerabilities, such as directory listings, using Google Dorking. Finally, create a threat intelligence mind map using XMap to visualize your findings.

Task Instructions

  1. Identify the Top 5 Enemy Nations of India
    • Use OSINT sources (e.g., news articles, government reports, geopolitical analyses) to determine the top 5 countries considered adversaries of India based on historical conflicts, recent cyberattacks, or geopolitical tensions.
    • Consider factors like state-sponsored cyberattacks, territorial disputes, or diplomatic tensions.
  2. Compile Government Websites
    • For each identified country, gather a list of official government websites (e.g., ministries, departments, or agencies).
    • Use search engines, WHOIS lookups, or official country portals to find domains with extensions like .gov, .gov.[country_code], or similar.
  3. Identify Vulnerabilities Using Google Dorking
    • Use the provided Google Dork query: intitle:"index of:" inurl:*gov.ttl to search for exposed directory listings on government websites.
    • Refine the query for each country by adding site:*.gov.[country_code] or specific keywords related to the country.
    • Document any vulnerabilities found, such as exposed files, directories, or sensitive information (e.g., configuration files, logs, or credentials).
    • Important: Do not attempt to access, modify, or exploit any vulnerabilities. This is for educational purposes only, and ethical guidelines must be followed.
  4. Create a Threat Intelligence Mind Map
    • Use XMap to create a visual mind map.
    • Structure the mind map with the following hierarchy for each country:
      • Country Name
        • List of Government Websites
        • Vulnerabilities (e.g., directory listings, exposed files)
    • Include annotations or notes on potential risks (e.g., data leaks, misconfigurations) based on your findings.
  5. Submission
    • Export your XMap mind map as a PDF or image.
    • Compile a report summarizing:
      • The top 5 enemy nations and your rationale for selecting them.
      • The list of government websites for each country.
      • Any vulnerabilities found via Google Dorking.
      • A screenshot or export of your XMap mind map.
    • Submit the report and mind map to complete the CTF challenge.

Google Dorking Hint

Use the following Google Dork query to find directory listings: intitle:"index of:" inurl:*gov.ttl

Example Refinement: To search for directory listings on Pakistani government websites, use: intitle:"index of:" inurl:*.gov.pk

Ethical Reminder: Google Dorking can reveal sensitive information. Use this technique responsibly and do not access or exploit any vulnerabilities. This CTF is for educational purposes only.
